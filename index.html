Research Vision

Building the next generation of human-centered intelligent systems

The laboratory studies how humans think, feel, decide, learn and interact with intelligent environments — and operates at the intersection of seven disciplines.

Artificial Intelligence Neuroscience Virtual Reality Human–Computer Interaction Cognitive Science Digital Twins Computational Psychology Multi-modal Sensor Fusion
Mission

To create intelligent environments capable of sensing, understanding, predicting and enhancing human experiences.

Grand Challenges

Five questions guiding our research

CHALLENGE 01

Emotionally Intelligent Machines

CHALLENGE 02

Cognitive Digital Twins

CHALLENGE 03

Adaptive Virtual Worlds

Leadership & research team

Lab Coordinator · Principal Investigator

Assoc. Prof. Gökhan İnce

Ph.D. in Computer Engineering · Istanbul Technical University, Faculty of Computer & Informatics Engineering

Dr. İnce leads the IBCS Lab, bridging the gap between computational systems and human neurophysiology. His research spans brain–computer interfacing, multi-modal sensor fusion, robot audition, affective computing and wearable technologies. He is founder/director of the User Experience Laboratory and co-founder/director of the Soft Sensors Laboratory at ITU.

“Transforming signals into human-centric solutions.”
